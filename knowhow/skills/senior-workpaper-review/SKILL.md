# Senior Workpaper Review

## Purpose

Use this skill to review a prepared TB bridge like a senior accountant. The goal is to catch defects and surface judgement clearly, not to reperform every row manually.

The client files are evidence. This skill is review guidance only. Never treat skill text as client evidence.

## Review Order

1. Check workbook controls.
2. Sample high-risk rows and movement columns.
3. Inspect original evidence for material or judgemental samples.
4. Check book/tax and valuation boundaries.
5. Check movement notes are clear enough for a junior accountant.
6. Decide whether corrections are needed or whether the workbook can be delivered with review notes.

## Control Checks

- Every movement column sums to zero.
- Every row reconciles: opening plus movements equals closing.
- P&L rows start from zero, not prior-year comparative values.
- Prior-FS opening balances are present and agree where possible.
- Evidence links are visible and usable.
- Relationship coverage is complete: ready, source-only, bank-only, excluded, and unresolved items are represented or explained.

## Risk-Based Sampling

Prioritise:

- Material cash movements.
- Investment sales and gain/loss calculations.
- Distribution receivable and source-only residuals.
- Beneficiary/UPE and owner distribution calculations.
- Related-party, loan, or internal transfer bridges.
- Bank-only expenses such as KPMG, ATO, fees, and unsupported payments.
- Accruals and prepayments with service-period cut-off.
- Tax-only components and valuation/NAV information.
- Rows marked judgement, unsupported, clearing, or needs attention.

## Review Judgement

Use “needs corrections” only when the workbook itself should change:

- mathematical control failure;
- unsupported posted amount;
- wrong book/tax boundary;
- wrong valuation treatment;
- prior FS opening row omitted;
- misleading classification;
- missing evidence status;
- presentation issue that could mislead the accountant.

Do not block merely because accountant judgement remains, if the judgement is clearly labelled and explained.

## Accounting Boundaries

- Franking credits, TFN withholding, tax offsets, and tax gross-ups are normally notes, not book postings.
- NAV and market values are normally notes, not book postings, unless fair value treatment is adopted.
- Beneficiary/UPE movement should be based on book-profit bridge unless another supported convention is documented.
- Bank-only classifications can be delivered when evidence is absent, provided missing support is clear.
- Wrong-entity or personal documents should be excluded unless the client confirms relevance.

## Reference Library

Read `references/senior-review-checklist.md` when reviewing a full TB/FS workpaper, a non-trust client, a new client type, or any workbook with significant judgement, tax, valuation, accrual, inventory, loan, owner/equity, or presentation risk.

For technical accounting uncertainty, use the `accounting-pdf-knowledge-retrieval` skill to consult the original CA knowhow PDF section. Use the retrieved section as review guidance only, then verify the workbook against client evidence.

## Correction Briefs

When a correction is needed, make it actionable:

- state the issue;
- state expected treatment;
- identify files, rows, columns, or amounts to recheck;
- describe the workbook change;
- give the validation test after correction.

Keep the accountant-facing message short and calm. The workbook is prepared for review, not final approval.
