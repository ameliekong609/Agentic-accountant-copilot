# Senior Review Checklist

Use this checklist as review guidance only. Client files, prior-year financial statements, and the generated workbook are the evidence.

## Must-Fix Controls

- Movement column does not net to zero.
- Row opening plus movements does not equal closing.
- Prior-FS balance-sheet row is missing without explanation.
- P&L row carries a prior-year amount as opening balance.
- Skill, knowhow, or training material is cited as evidence.
- Tax-only or valuation-only item is posted without explicit book-treatment support.
- Evidence link is invisible or unusable.

## Review-But-Can-Deliver Items

- Judgement is clearly labelled and explained.
- Support is missing but the row is marked needs attention.
- Bank-only item is classified with missing invoice/support shown.
- Source-only item is noted as receivable/payable/accrual candidate.
- Market value/NAV is noted but not posted.

## Sampling Focus

- Largest cash movements.
- Investment sales and gain/loss.
- Source-only residuals and receivables.
- Accruals, prepayments, prior-year clearances.
- Related-party, loan, owner, beneficiary, dividend, drawings, or equity movements.
- Tax/GST/ATO/PAYG/ASIC movements.
- Rows where AI used judgement or unsupported status.
- Any extension_role or new accounting role proposal.

## Cross-Client Review Lens

- Companies: dividends, retained earnings, tax payable, directors/shareholders loans, share capital.
- Trusts: UPE, beneficiary distribution, investment income, tax components.
- Trading businesses: inventory, COGS, debtors, creditors, GST, payroll.
- Service businesses: revenue cut-off, WIP, prepayments, accrued expenses.
- Investment entities: cost vs fair value, distributions, realised gains/losses, source-only entitlements.
- Property entities: rental income, agent statements, loan interest, capex vs repairs.

## Senior Review Output

- Keep findings actionable.
- Separate workbook defects from accountant judgement.
- If correction is needed, say exactly what Tessa must change and how to validate it.
- Do not block because judgement remains if the workbook is clear and controlled.
